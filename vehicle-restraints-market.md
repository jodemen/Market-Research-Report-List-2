<p><p>Vehicle restraints are devices used to secure vehicles in place during transportation. There are three main types of vehicle restraints: hydraulic, electric, and mechanical. Hydraulic restraints use hydraulic pressure to lock vehicles in place, providing strong and reliable security. Electric restraints use electricity to secure vehicles, allowing for quick and easy operation. Mechanical restraints use physical mechanisms such as clamps or chocks to hold vehicles securely. Each type of restraint offers unique advantages and is chosen based on specific requirements and preferences.</p></p>
