<p><p>Composite tooling refers to molds or tools used in the production of composite materials. There are two main types of composite tooling materials: glass fiber and carbon fiber. Glass fiber composites are known for their cost-effectiveness, ease of use, and versatility in a wide range of industries. On the other hand, carbon fiber composites offer superior strength, stiffness, and lightweight properties, making them ideal for high-performance applications such as aerospace and automotive industries. Both materials play a crucial role in the manufacturing of composite parts.</p></p>
