<p><ul><li>External Heated Soldering Iron</li><li>Internal Heated Soldering Iron</li></ul></p>
<p><p>External Heated Soldering Irons use an external heat source, typically a flame or electric stove, to heat the tip of the soldering iron. These are commonly used in industrial settings where high temperatures are needed. Internal Heated Soldering Irons have a heating element built into the tip of the iron itself, allowing for quicker and more precise heating. These are often used in electronics and fine soldering applications. Both types cater to different needs and preferences in the electric soldering iron market.</p></p>
