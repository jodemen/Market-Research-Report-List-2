<p><p>Military Unmanned Underwater Vehicles (UUVs) can be categorized into two main types: Remotely Operated Vehicles (ROVs) and Autonomous Underwater Vehicles (AUVs). ROVs are controlled by an operator at the surface through a tether, allowing for real-time monitoring and intervention. AUVs, on the other hand, operate independently using pre-programmed instructions, sensors, and algorithms. AUVs are ideal for long-duration missions in challenging environments where constant human supervision is not practical. Both types play crucial roles in military applications for surveillance, reconnaissance, mine countermeasures, and research operations.</p></p>
